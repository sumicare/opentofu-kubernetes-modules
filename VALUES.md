## Core Values

 - Brutal honesty always wins over hypocritical politeness 🪓
 - We are not special, we are not unique, we are not irreplaceable 🫥
 - We are building a viable product, not a dream 🛠️
 - Everything must be validated, not just speculated 🔍
 - We are changing the rules, "good" is always not enough 📏

Any viable organization needs:
 - Escalation chain – issues are escalated quickly, with clear actions and measurable outcomes ⚡️
 - Subordination chain – everyone knows who they report to and what they are responsible for 🧭
 - Delegation chain – decisions are delegated to people with the capability and capacity to reach the desired outcome 🎯

### Constructive Behavior

 - We do not tolerate unaccountable behavior - when accountability negated and replaced with superficial collaboration detraction comes. 
 - By staying accountable and fostering genuine constructive collaboration, we build transparency, trust and momentum.
 - [Counterproductive work behaviour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Counterproductive_work_behavior) and [Organization Deviance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organization_deviance) make any Agile practices dysfunctional, at best.
 - Replacing process with ceremony obliterates product value.
 - Best practices remain paperweight without discipline and clear intent.
 - Spreading mediocrity was never a strategy.

### Essential Competency and Validated Proficiency 🧠

We assume a "default competency" for each project and expect folks to:
 - Know Go at least at the [tour of go](https://tour.golang.org/) level 🐹
 - Know Node.js; with OpenJS certification discontinued, any stack is fine as long as it is tested more than "good enough" 🟢
 - Have [Terraform Associate](https://developer.hashicorp.com/certifications/infrastructure-automation) level understanding 🧱
 - Have [LFCS](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/certification/linux-foundation-certified-sysadmin-lfcs/) skills to administer Linux and write shell scripts, prep container images 🐧
 - Have basic [LFCA](https://training.linuxfoundation.org/certification/certified-it-associate/) knowledge to understand how management works and what GitOps is 🧾
 - Hold [CKAD](https://www.cncf.io/certification/ckad/) to develop applications using common Kubernetes resources 📦
 - Hold [CKA](https://www.cncf.io/certification/cka/) for Kubernetes administration 🛳️
 - Hold [CKS](https://www.cncf.io/certification/cks/) for supply chain and cluster security 🔐

We do not expect people to pass all of these on demand or keep every certification current; certificates alone are worthless. We expect relevant proficiency, or the ability to gain relevant insight when needed.

Mental capacity and cognitive-complexity thresholds differ for everyone. We do not expect people "to know everything" "just because" – we validate proficiency.

When you put people onto an AWS-hosted project, an [AWS Developer Associate](https://aws.amazon.com/training/certifications/developer-associate/) level helps avoid dangerous mistakes
(for example, hardcoding credentials instead of using AWS STS, or sharing production data with staging).

### Security & Compliance 🛡️

Everything is encrypted and prepared for GDPR/BDSG/TTDSG compliance 🔐

Most of the conventions presented here are still rare even in multi-million-dollar "enterprise-grade" projects, often due to negligence and paperweight certifications 💸
We gladly accept constructive feedback and criticism; we care about continuous improvement, not continuous idiocy and degradation — CI/CD defines existence for us ♻️

**We Don't** publish the most essential details, for a reason.

**We Don't** believe in security through obscurity, so you will still have to do your own homework.

Good starting points for understanding our expectations are:
 
 - SOC1 – Financial audit run over ~6 months (Type 2), with a public report confirming the company isn’t imploding from fraud or broken controls. Baseline street cred so others will do KYB and still talk to you. 📊
 - SOC2 – Non‑public security report that details how PII is locked down and how leaks are prevented/detected, also over ~6 months (Type 2). Shared with customers, partners, and private auditors; treated as a continuous process, with on‑demand reports for people who care when things hit the fan. 🧾
 - SOC3 – Public, simplified SOC2 for the general crowd. Fewer reporting requirements, more marketing, but it at least signals the company takes security somewhat seriously. 📣
 - [ISO20000-1](https://www.iso.org/standard/70636.html) – Service Management System (SMS) requirements for ITSM: roles, policies, authorities, and how resource management is applied. 🏗️
 - [ISO22301](https://www.iso.org/standard/75106.html) – Business Continuity Management System (BCMS) requirements: how risk management and continuity planning are applied. 🔄
 - [ISO27001](https://www.iso.org/standard/27001) – Information Security Management System (ISMS) requirements: risk assessment and treatment, complementary to business continuity. 🧱
 - [ISO27017](https://www.iso.org/standard/82878.html) – Cloud security controls: formalized security requirements for cloud infrastructures. ☁️
 - [ISO27018](https://www.iso.org/standard/27018) – PII protection: formalized privacy and access-management requirements for cloud infrastructures. 🧬
 - [ISO27701](https://www.iso.org/standard/27701) – Privacy Information Management System (PIMS): access control and breach-detection requirements. 🚨
 - [ISO42001](https://www.iso.org/standard/42001) – Artificial Intelligence Management System (AIMS), aligned with the [EU AI Act](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/), so your models stay within ethical and legal boundaries 🤖
 - [ISO9001](https://www.iso.org/standard/88464.html) – Quality Management System (QMS) requirements: basic proof your team is not in a vegetative state. 🧠
